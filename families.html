Looking for fun for the family? Try These!

U.S. Space and Rocket Center

Spend a day out of this world at the U.S. Space & Rocket Center. People of all generations can feel like an astronaut for the day or the week! See the National Historic Landmark - an authentic Saturn V moon rocket - one of only three on display in the world! Explore the development and evolution of the Space Shuttle program to the International Space Station and learn about NASA's latest innovations. Stay an hour or stay a week… there is something for everyone!

Huntsville Botanical Garden

Open year-round, the Huntsville Botanical Garden contains diverse ecosystems to explore within its 118 acres. From grassy meadows to woodland paths, aquatic habitats to stunning floral collections, the Garden invites guests of all ages to discover the beauty and wonder of the natural environment. Play freely in the Children's Garden after encountering the fluttering butterflies in the nation's largest open-air butterfly house. With additional events, exhibits, and programs throughout the year, the Garden is a source of plant conservation, education, and celebration for all.

Cathedral Caverns State Park

Alabama's "Coolest" Underground Adventure. Travel 1.3 miles thru one of the largest show caves in the U.S. Marvel over the huge opening leading into the cave, experience the beauty of the stalagmite formations, hear the gurgling sound of water from Mystery River. See a prehistoric shark's tooth embedded in the ceiling and look closely for the bats scattered throughout.

1818 Farms

Events of all types are hosted at 1818 Farms. Our annual "Sheep Shearing Day" kicks off the spring season in late March. Tours and "Open Farms Days" are available April through June. The Garden House is also home to a series of classes including: raised bed gardening, seed starting, raising backyard chickens, wreath making and flower preparation and arranging. Additionally, the landscape of 1818 Farms makes a beautiful backdrop for special event photo shoots. Visitors to the farms can also purchase 1818 Farms' bath and beauty products.

Belle Mont Mansion

Built circa 1828, Belle Mont Mansion is one of the most outstanding Palladian-style houses in the Deep South. The historic home is open for guided tours and hosts an annual quilt show in October, a "Plantation Christmas" celebration in December, and other living history events throughout the year. Belle Mont is a historic property of the Alabama Historical Commission. Currently, the Colbert County Historical Landmarks Foundation operates this historic home and is offering special programsto increase awareness and to raise funds to maintain the site.

Cook Museam of Natural Science

Touch a meteorite from outer space. Climb to the top of Big Tree. Be mesmerized by moon jellies. Build a volcano and watch it erupt. Life is AMAZING at Cook Museum! The 62,000 square foot Cook Museum offers an interactive immersion into North American Biomes, from deserts to oceans, to arctic tundra to hardwood forests. Key features of the new Museum include: Educational programs, live animals & aquariums, Nature's Table café with outdoor patio, three classrooms, and Museum Store.

Huntsville Symphony Orchestra

HSO has a FREE family concert every year along with a Music Explorers Club for families. During Music Explorers Club meetings, we learn all about music and instruments and even make some music of our own. Become a seasoned traveler and fill your Music Explorer Passport with six musical instrument stamps to win a special PRIZE! Best of all, membership in the Music Explorers Club is FREE to children and families. Meetings happen year-round — be sure to keep an eye out for upcoming Music Explorers Club meetings!

Lake Guntersville State Park

The park features a lodge with 112 hotel rooms and suites, 20 chalets, 15 cottages, a full-service restaurant and an 18-hole golf course. Camping and cabins are available on the lake. Enjoy more than 30 miles of hiking trails, picnicking, fabulous vistas and numerous sites for viewing wildlife. It is a winter home of the American bald eagle and the Park features educational programs.

Red Bay Water Park

The Red Bay Water Park is open daily beginning Memorial Day weekend thru mid-August. Price of admission is $8 per swimmer and season passes are available. Hours of operation are M-F Noon until 5:00 and Sunday 1:00 until 5:00. Private parties are also available. The Red Bay Family Fun Park includes a pavilion, picnic areas, skateboard park and a playground area located next to the Water Park. Also located at the Family Fun Park is the Hoyt Keeton Walking Trail, which is a quarter-mile paved path that circles the park.
